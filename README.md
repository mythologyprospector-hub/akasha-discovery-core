# Akasha Discovery Core

Akasha Discovery Core is the primary exploration engine of the Akasha ecosystem.

Its purpose is to analyze knowledge structures, identify gaps, and generate hypotheses for further investigation.

This system implements the Akasha Discovery Doctrine defined in the akasha-axioms repository.

---

## Purpose

Discovery Core attempts to answer a simple question:

"What knowledge should exist but does not yet?"

The system explores relationships between known concepts and attempts to identify missing connections.

---

## Core Concepts

### Knowledge Graphs

Concepts are represented as nodes in a graph.

Relationships between concepts form edges.

Discovery occurs when the system detects:

• missing edges  
• unstable clusters  
• incomplete domains  
• unexplored adjacency relationships

---

### Constraint Analysis

Scientific domains are constrained systems.

Discovery Core analyzes constraints across domains to detect patterns where missing structures may exist.

Examples include:

• physics phase spaces  
• chemical periodicity  
• mathematical symmetries  
• biological taxonomies

---

### Hypothesis Generation

When structural gaps are detected, the system generates candidate hypotheses.

These hypotheses are recorded and may later be tested through simulation or experiment.

---

## Role in Akasha

Akasha Discovery Core feeds the ecosystem.

discovery → forge → constellation

Discovery identifies unknowns.

Forge builds tools to investigate them.

Constellation records the evolving structure of the system.

---

## Philosophy

Discovery does not claim truth.

It proposes questions.

Human stewards evaluate those questions and guide further investigation.

Akasha Discovery Core exists to help humans explore knowledge more effectively.
